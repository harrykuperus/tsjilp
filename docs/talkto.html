The Problem

Humans are not very good at text communication. In real life we rely on tone, gestures, and immediate feedback to avoid misunderstandings. Text conversations remove those signals.

The result is familiar to everyone:

  • long message threads
  • misunderstandings
  • lost context
  • forgotten decisions

ChatACM helps people communicate more clearly by adding personal assistants to conversations.

How it Works

Human Conversation

People communicate normally. The conversation stays simple and human.

Private Assistants

Each participant has a personal assistant that helps privately by explaining messages, suggesting replies, and summarizing discussions.

Conversation Memory

Assistants remember contacts, decisions, open questions and important information so nothing gets lost in the conversation history.

Example

Conversation:

Emanuel ● The contact person is Matteo Morzenti.
Harry ● Perfect, I will contact him to schedule the visit.

Harry's assistant (private):

Contact detected: Matteo Morzenti
Next step: Schedule the site visit.
Suggested reply: Buongiorno Matteo...

The assistant helps privately, but the conversation itself remains clean.

Transparent Assistance

ChatACM avoids the "black box AI" problem. Every message shows where it comes from.

  • human message
  • assistant suggestion
  • imported email or chat message

Users can inspect messages using Show Origin to see how they were created.

The E-Bike Principle

ChatACM does not replace human communication.

It works like an e-bike: you still do the work, but the system helps where things become difficult.

  • clarifies messages
  • suggests replies
  • tracks decisions
  • summarizes conversations
  • remembers context

Founder Whitepaper

A concise product narrative suitable for developers, early partners, and potential investors.

Introduction

Digital communication tools today generate enormous volumes of messages but do little to help people manage conversations effectively. Important information becomes buried in message history, misunderstandings arise easily, and decisions are difficult to track. Humans communicate best through tone, gesture, and immediate feedback, but text-based communication removes these signals and forces people to repeatedly clarify or search through previous discussions.

TalkTo introduces a new communication layer where conversations are supported by intelligent assistants that help keep discussions organized, clear, and productive. Instead of replacing people with AI, TalkTo introduces assistants that facilitate conversations by remembering context, summarizing progress, and highlighting when human attention is required.

Core Concept

TalkTo provides shared conversation spaces accessible through simple links such as talkto.me/harry. Anyone with the link can join a conversation. Participants may include real people and their personal assistants.

Assistants support the conversation but never replace the participants. Humans remain the final decision makers.

The Role of the Assistant

The assistant helps by:

  • remembering previous discussions
  • summarizing progress
  • translating languages
  • detecting misunderstandings
  • highlighting unresolved topics
  • notifying users when input is required

The assistant acts as a conversation facilitator, not a decision maker.

Conversation Memory

TalkTo assistants maintain a structured memory of each conversation. Instead of relying on long message histories, users can ask simple questions such as:

  • What was the last quote?
  • Did we discuss heating?
  • What topics are still unresolved?

Automatic Topic Detection

Real conversations often involve multiple topics at the same time, but traditional messaging systems force everything into a single timeline. TalkTo assistants automatically detect and organize topics such as price, delivery, specifications, installation, and open technical questions.

Decision Tracking

Assistants identify and record decisions made during conversations, such as confirmed price, accepted delivery, or agreed specifications. This prevents confusion and ensures that agreements remain clearly documented.

Conversation State Detection

Assistants detect the state of each discussion, including question asked, answer provided, clarification needed, waiting for response, agreement reached, and resolved. This allows the system to understand progress and determine what action may be required next.

Attention Management

Modern messaging platforms generate constant notifications, which quickly become overwhelming. TalkTo introduces attention signals that notify users only when their input is required.

  • waiting for you
  • waiting for the other participant
  • decision required
  • resolved

Conversation Map

Instead of only showing a message timeline, TalkTo can present discussions as a map of topics and decisions, allowing users to navigate directly to the part of the conversation that matters.

Conversation Time Machine

Assistants identify key moments in conversations and store them as events such as topic created, question asked, quote received, decision made, and topic resolved. Users can jump directly to important moments instead of scrolling through message history.

Misunderstanding Detection

Because text communication lacks tone and immediate correction, misunderstandings are common. Assistants help detect possible ambiguity and encourage clarification using careful, neutral language.

Private Assistant Channel

Each user has a private conversation with their assistant where summaries, unresolved issues, response suggestions, and attention notifications can be presented without interrupting the main discussion.

Personal Conversation Memory

TalkTo can also act as a personal conversation memory system. The assistant can extract summaries, tasks, decisions, and unresolved questions from email threads, chats, and project discussions.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

  • personal conversation link
  • shared chat interface
  • assistant participation
  • conversation memory
  • topic detection
  • decision tracking
  • private assistant channel
  • attention signals when input is required

Design Principles

  • Human First: Assistants support people but never replace them.
  • Clarity Over Speed: Better conversations matter more than faster conversations.
  • Attention Filtering: Users should only be notified when their attention is needed.
  • Model Independence: The system should work with multiple AI providers.
  • User Ownership: Users retain control over identity and conversation data.

Vision

Digital communication today produces enormous amounts of messages but very little structured knowledge. TalkTo introduces a new communication layer where assistants help structure, remember, and clarify discussions.

Instead of simply sending messages, participants gain a conversation environment where important information remains accessible, misunderstandings are reduced, decisions become clear, and conversations become easier to manage.

The long-term vision is a communication system where conversations naturally transform into structured knowledge while remaining human-centered.
